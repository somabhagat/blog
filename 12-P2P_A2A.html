P2P: The Application Layer & The SPL "Address Book"

Person-to-Person (P2P) describes a specific application: transferring money between individuals using an easy "Proxy" like a phone number or email instead of a 22-digit IBAN. The technical secret to making this work cross-border is SPL (SEPA Proxy Lookup).

Managed by the European Payments Council (EPC), SPL is the "address book" that connects fragmented national apps like TWINT (Switzerland), Swish (Sweden), and Bizum (Spain) without needing a single shared database. It is a Central Alias Directory mechanism that maps a phone number to an IBAN through an intelligent routing network.

🔍
SPL Lookup: When you send money, your bank (Initiating Registry Provider - IRP) sends a query to the SPL hub (operated by equensWorldline). SPL doesn't store the IBAN; it queries the Responding Registry Provider (RRP) in the recipient's country in real-time.
🛡️
Separation of Concerns: SPL handles discovery (the "where"), but the movement (the "how") happens over existing rails like SEPA Instant. It breaks the silos, allowing a "national habit" like TWINT to eventually talk to the rest of the Eurozone.

A2A: The Infrastructure Layer & The MDR Reset

To understand why A2A is a structural disruptor, we have to look at the legacy costs it replaces. In a traditional card transaction, merchants pay a percentage-based Merchant Discount Rate (MDR). Think of the MDR as the "super umbrella" for the total cost of acceptance.

Under this umbrella hides the Card Fee "Triple Threat" that A2A effectively dismantles:

  • Interchange Fee — The largest component, paid to the customer’s bank (the Issuer) to cover risk and credit costs.
  • Acquirer Markup — The fee paid to the merchant’s bank for processing and settlement services.
  • Scheme Fees — The "invisible tax" paid to the card network (the Scheme) for the privilege of using their global infrastructure and brand.

This is where the logic of the "Card Killer" becomes undeniable. In an A2A transaction, those billion-dollar scheme fees are eliminated. Because the money flows directly from Bank A to Bank B, it never touches a card network—meaning the "Scheme" has no role and, more importantly, no fee to collect.

For perspective, the top two global card networks—Visa and Mastercard—reported combined annual revenues exceeding $72.8 billion in 2025. A significant portion of this revenue is derived from the service and data processing fees charged on every transaction that crosses their rails.

However, it is a mistake to assume the "Schemes" are simply waiting for A2A to take over. While their traditional transaction processing margins face pressure, their revenue streams have increasingly shifted toward value-added services. In 2025, segments such as tokenization, fraud prevention, and strategic consulting grew at a significantly faster rate than basic processing fees. This pivot suggests the networks are successfully re-positioning themselves as data and security platforms, effectively de-risking their business model against the rise of real-time A2A rails.

A2A shifts this paradigm. Instead of a percentage of total volume being swallowed by the MDR umbrella, merchants pay a low fixed fee (often just pennies) to an Open Banking provider (a PISP). This transforms payments from a variable tax on growth into a predictable, flat-rate infrastructure cost.

Decoding the ISO 20022 Syntax: The DNA of A2A

In the A2A ecosystem, every transaction follows a strict four-part dot-notation. Understanding this is the difference between "vibe coding" and engineering a resilient payment rail:

1. The Category (e.g., pain, pacs, camt): Defines the "neighborhood." PAIN is for Payment Initiation (Customer-to-Bank), PACS for Payment Clearing and Settlement (Bank-to-Bank), and CAMT for Cash Management - reporting.

2. The Message Function (e.g., 001,002, 008): Defines the specific "job." 001 is the instruction to pay, 002 is a status report and 008 is the actual interbank movement of funds.

3. The Variant (e.g., 001): Defines the specific "flavor" of the message. While 001 is the global standard, other variants exist for specific local markets or regulatory silos.

4. The Version (e.g., 09, 08, 10): Defines the schema release. Newer versions like pacs.002.001.10 are mandatory for 2026 because they carry the granular "Reason Codes" needed for high-speed P2P feedback loops.

The Regulatory Hammer: IPR Timeline & Fee Parity

The Instant Payments Regulation (IPR) has officially weaponized these rails for retail. As of October 9, 2025, the "Instant Premium" was officially removed for Eurozone banks. If a standard transfer is free, the 10-second version must be too. This removes the final barrier for A2A to become the "new normal" for European retail payments.

Did you know the Price of Speed?

Before the 2025 mandate, "Instant" wasn't just a technical choice—it was a luxury product. While the actual interbank technical cost to a bank is roughly €0.002 per transaction, consumer fees typically ranged from €0.50 to €1.50. In less competitive markets like Spain or Italy, some banks charged as much as €15.00 for a single transfer, effectively using price to protect legacy card revenues.

The Infrastructure Reality Check

Payments Insider Edition

Settlement Finality: The "no-take-backs" rule of the A2A world. While card schemes allow a 120-day window for the "I don't recognize this" claim, A2A settlement is technically and legally irrevocable the moment it hits the ledger. It turns a one-way street into a merchant’s best friend.

The "Metadata Engine" (ISO 20022): More than just XML tags; it’s the rich data layer that turns a generic transfer into contextual commerce. It provides the structured breadcrumbs that allow AI agents to auto-reconcile a thousand invoices before your coffee gets cold.

The Ad Valorem Tax (MDR): Legacy card pricing is a tax on your growth—the more you sell, the more they take. A2A breaks this cycle by swapping a variable percentage for a flat-rate utility fee, treating payments like the infrastructure they actually are.

Soft Decline: The financial equivalent of "it’s not you, it’s the network." It is the most optimistic form of failure, suggesting the funds exist but the pipes are temporarily too congested to prove it. 📉

The 3DS Friction Gap: That digital obstacle course that kills conversion for a €10 item. By leveraging native banking biometrics (SCA) directly on the device, A2A offers the security of a card vault without the manual code-entry nightmare.
