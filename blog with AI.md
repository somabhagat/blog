## 2. Content Strategy & Personal Branding

**Question:** *Can I afford to create a LinkedIn post like this guy — funny, punchy or will it dilute the authority and trust since he is a CEO and I am unemployed? I am thinking maybe this is something for later when I have a credible role title on my LinkedIn profile.*

**Response:** In 2026, **Insight + Personality > Role Title**. Your 13 years of experience at a giant like Mastercard is your "Trust Anchor." That doesn't go away because you are between roles. In fact, being "punchy" while unemployed can actually make you look more confident, like an expert who knows their value so well they don't need to hide behind a corporate suit.

### Inspiration: Style & Tone
Here is the example referenced from **Max Shevlyakov** (Co-Founder at Finalyst):

> **15 terms everyone in payments should know (No.9 is my favourite.) 😅**
> 
> 1. **Acquirer** – the merchant's bank. The middleman nobody talks about.
> 2. **Issuer** – the customer's bank. The one that says yes or no.
> 3. **Interchange** – the fee that travels invisibly with every card swipe. You never see it. It's always there.
> 4. **Chargeback** – when the customer calls their bank and says, "I didn't do it." Merchants cry.
> 5. **PCI DSS** – a long compliance checklist that makes everyone nervous.
> 6. **Settlement** – when the money finally, actually moves. Usually T+1 or T+2. Patience.
> 7. **Authorisation** – the card network asking, "Got the funds?" and the issuer saying, "Maybe."
> 8. **Tokenisation** – your card number in a witness protection program.
> 9. **Payment orchestration** – having a backup plan for your backup plan.
> 10. **A2A payments** – skipping Visa and Mastercard entirely. They're not happy about it.
> 11. **ISO 20022** – XML-based messaging standard. Thrilling at parties.
> 12. **Scheme** – Visa and Mastercard. The landlords of card payments.
> 13. **MDR** – the small cut the merchant never fully understood when they signed up.
> 14. **3DS** – the extra step that makes you find your phone to complete a €4 purchase.
> 15. **Open Banking** – your bank finally sharing your data. Reluctantly.
> 16. **Soft decline** – “not now.” The most optimistic kind of failure.
> 17. **Hard decline** – “not ever.” Brutal, but honest.
> 20. **Scheme fees** - fees that are invisible for merchants and unpredictable for charging from issuing and acquiring sides.
> 21. **BIN** - the first 6-8 digits that decide your fate before the transaction even starts.
> 22. **FX markup** - where “competitive rates” quietly disappear.
> 23. **Blacklisting** - one mistake, permanent memory.
> 24. **Transaction ID** - unique identifier that every bank, issuer, PSP and company with any say in the whole flow generates in a different way and format, helping them point at each other when customers starts to get angry and asking questions about where their money is. Cause who needs ARN or any other standardised trash.
> 25. **KYC** - when merchant or bank requires you to prove your existence.
> 26. **Callback or webhook** - when another system telling another system a final status of a transaction.
> 27. **Reversal** - when you make a wrong transaction and asking to issuer bank to reverse the money.
> 28. **PAN / FPAN / DPAN** - because remembering only one number is too easy. Oh you've memorised those ones? Here's a flexible credential as well then.

### Why This Style Works 
Max isn't just being funny; he’s doing three very specific things that you can "borrow" safely:

1. **Shared Industry Trauma:** He jokes about things only "insiders" understand (e.g., ISO 20022 being boring, or the pain of 3DS). This builds a "tribe."
2. **The "Hook" (No. 9 is my favorite):** This forces people to scroll down, increasing "Dwell Time," which the algorithm loves.
3. **Low Friction:** It’s a listicle. It’s easy to read in 20 seconds.

### Applying This to Your Profile
You don't have to be a "joker." You can be the **"Expert with an Edge."** Here are three safe ways to adapt his USP for your situation:

- **The "Insider Truths" List (Authority-First):** Instead of defining terms, list "5 Technical Truths about [Topic] that the Sales Deck misses."
  * **Why it works:** Shows you have been "in the room." Reinforces your technical background (IHK/Mastercard) but uses Max's punchy style.
  * **Tone:** "I’ve seen the backend of this, and here is the reality."

- **The "Dual-Profile" Hook:** Use your current status as a USP.
  * **Draft Hook:** "I’ve spent 13 years in Payments Technology and 6 months deep-diving into AI Agents. Here are 5 things I realized about the gap between 'Payment Regulation' and 'Actual Code'..."
  * **Why it works:** Frames your "unemployed" time as a "Research & Specialization" phase. Turns a perceived weakness into a "Dual-Profile" strength.

- **The "Translation" Post:** Take a dry regulatory update and "translate" it into "Human."
  * **Draft Hook:** "The EU just released 400 pages on PSD3. I read it so you don't have to. Here is the 'TL;DR' version for Product Managers:"
  * **Why it works:** This is the highest form of authority. If you can simplify something complex, you prove you own the subject matter.
